The "call," in plain English

These are callable bonds. One feature, one trade-off — here's the whole story.

1
You earn a higher coupon. Because you're giving the issuer a choice (below), they pay you more than a plain bond of the same maturity.
2
The issuer can buy it back early. If interest rates fall, they may "call" the bond — return your principal and stop the coupon. That's the only catch.
3
Either outcome is fine for you. Not called → you keep collecting an above-market coupon. Called → you get 100¢ back and redeploy. You were paid extra the whole time for the possibility.
✓
Bottom line: you're paid a premium yield to let the issuer refinance if rates drop. For a buy-and-earn investor, that premium is the point.
