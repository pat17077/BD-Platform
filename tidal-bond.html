The risks, straight

📞

Call risk — the main one

If rates fall, the issuer may call it early. You get your principal back at 100¢ and stop earning the high coupon — then you reinvest at the now-lower rates. You're paid the premium coupon precisely for taking this.

🏛️

Credit risk — very low

These are US federal agencies (FHLB, Freddie, Farm Credit) — among the highest-quality issuers in the world, a notch below US Treasuries. Default risk is minimal.

🌊

Liquidity risk

If you want out before maturity, you sell on Tidal — peer-to-peer, into dealer bids during US hours, or to us up to a size after hours. Pricing moves with the market; you may get more or less than you paid.

📈

Rate risk

If you sell before maturity and rates have risen, the price is lower (and vice-versa). Hold to call or maturity and you get par back regardless.
